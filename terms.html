4. Safety Instructions — special rules for Iron-Mode, Cold-Exposure, Heat-Exposure

4.1 General breath-work safety (all Breathwork modes)

  • Never practice any breath retention technique (holding your breath for more than 10 seconds) while standing unsupported, in or near water, while driving, cycling, or operating any machinery or vehicle
  • Always practice seated or lying on a stable surface
  • Stop immediately if you experience discomfort, chest pain, or visual disturbances

Mild tingling in the hands or feet and slight lightheadedness are common responses to controlled breathing techniques. However, stop immediately if you experience: strong muscle cramping or spasms, numbness in face or limbs, chest tightness, heart palpitations, or loss of visual clarity. These may indicate excessive hyperventilation requiring medical attention.

4.2 Iron-Mode (experimental ANS strain cockpit)

Iron-Mode is an experimental training mode that displays sympathetic-activation and recovery-status indicators based on HRV trends. Indicators are not diagnoses. Do not rely on Iron-Mode to assess overtraining, fitness-for-competition or health status. Combine with established training-load methodology and qualified coaching/medical supervision.

4.3 Cold-Exposure (ice baths, cold plunges, cold showers)

Cold-Exposure modes are used at your own risk. Cold-shock response can trigger arrhythmia, vasovagal syncope, hypothermia and drowning. Mandatory rules:

  • Never alone in cold water — always have a sober buddy or trained supervisor present. Drowning from cold-shock syncope is the leading risk.
  • Never combine breath-retention or hyperventilation with cold-water immersion.
  • Stop and warm up immediately at: persistent shivering you cannot voluntarily stop, dizziness, chest pain, numbness lasting beyond exit, confusion or speech changes.
  • Start with short durations (30–60 sec), conservative temperatures (12–15 °C), and increase gradually only after multiple uneventful sessions.
  • Do not use Cold-Exposure if you have cardiovascular conditions, uncontrolled hypertension, Raynaud's syndrome, cold-urticaria, severe asthma, pregnancy, recent injury, or are under the influence of alcohol, sedatives or CNS-affecting medications.

4.4 Heat-Exposure (sauna, heat training)

Heat-Exposure modes are used at your own risk. Heat stress can trigger hyperthermia, heat-stroke, dehydration and cardiovascular collapse. Mandatory rules:

  • Hydrate before and after every session.
  • Stop and cool down immediately at: dizziness, headache, nausea, muscle cramps, confusion, cessation of sweating, palpitations.
  • Do not combine with alcohol, intense breath retention or hard physical exertion in the same session.
  • Never use Heat-Exposure alone if you are inexperienced — sauna fatality risk is dominated by syncope/falls in unsupervised settings.
  • Do not use Heat-Exposure if you have cardiovascular conditions, uncontrolled hypertension, are pregnant, recently underwent surgery, have skin conditions worsened by heat, severe psoriasis flares, or take medications that impair thermoregulation.

4.5 Recommended safety setup on Apple Watch

  • Enable Fall Detection: Settings → Emergency SOS → Fall Detection
  • Set up Medical ID: Health app → Medical ID
  • Inform a trusted person before practicing breath retention, Cold- or Heat-Exposure alone

Note: Settings paths may vary by watchOS version.

In case of loss of consciousness or severe distress during practice, call emergency services immediately (EU: 112, US: 911). Never practice alone if you are attempting breath retention techniques exceeding 30 seconds or any Cold-Water immersion.
